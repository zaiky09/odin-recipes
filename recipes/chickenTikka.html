Chicken Tikka

Description

Chicken tikka is a popular Indian dish made by marinating small pieces of boneless chicken in a mixture of yogurt, spices, and lemon juice. The marinated chicken is then grilled or baked until tender and slightly charred, giving it a smoky flavor. It's often served with naan, rice, or as a component in dishes like chicken tikka masala. The dish is known for its vibrant red color and aromatic spices.

Ingredients

Marinade:

  • Chicken thighs or breasts, boneless and skinless
  • Yogurt
  • Olive oil
  • Lemon juice
  • Garlic, minced
  • Ground coriander
  • Ground paprika
  • Ground black pepper
  • Salt

Serving:

  • Naan or flatbread
  • Fresh vegetables (lettuce, tomatoes, cucumbers, red onions)
  • Pickles
  • Fresh parsley
  • Tamarind sauce or garlic sauce

Steps

  1. Combine Ingredients: In a bowl, mix together yogurt, olive oil, lemon juice, minced garlic, ground coriander, ground paprika, ground black pepper, and salt.
  2. Marinate the Chicken: Add the boneless, skinless chicken thighs or breasts to the marinade, ensuring they are well-coated. Cover and refrigerate for at least 1 hour, preferably overnight for the best flavor.
  3. Preheat Oven or Grill: Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C) or prepare a grill for medium-high heat.
  4. Oven Method: Place the marinated chicken on a baking sheet and bake for 25-30 minutes, or until fully cooked, turning halfway through.
  5. Rest and Slice: Allow the cooked chicken to rest for a few minutes, then slice it thinly.
  6. Chop Vegetables: Slice fresh vegetables like lettuce, tomatoes, cucumbers, and red onions.
  7. Add fresh vegetables, pickles, and parsley.
  8. Drizzle Sauces: Drizzle sauce over the top. Add hummus if desired.
